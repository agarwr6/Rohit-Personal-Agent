# About Rohit — Career Goals, Work Style, Job Search

## Career story (in his words)
Two parts. First ~8 years in the Dell family: started as a co-op at EMC in 2016, converted full-time, moved to Virtustream, then Dell's ARC group (ISG org) as a data engineering consultant — building the data foundation (finance, revenue, server/storage/networking) and making data ready for analytics and ML teams. Realized he had a strong data engineering foundation but had never been customer-facing, so joined Atlan as a Solutions Architect. Atlan is a metadata governance catalog shifting toward context engineering for AI agents. Day-to-day: get on a call with enterprise customers, understand the problem, architect and build the solution.

## What Rohit wants next
Move to the intersection of data readiness and AI enablement — closer to the data layer, with **ownership of production systems**. In the SA role he builds a solution and hands it off, then moves on; he never owns anything in production or sees the long-term real-world impact. He wants to run a system in production and improve it over time, and to broaden beyond the single metadata/governance lens to the full AI space. Rough split he likes: ~70% AI/data enablement, ~30% client-facing solutioning.

## Target roles
Solution Architect, Forward Deployed Engineer, Data Engineer, Analytics Engineer (also AI Solutions Architect / AI Data Specialist). The common thread: the intersection of data engineering and AI enablement.

## Location
Austin, TX. Open to anywhere in the US.

## Work style & values
- Doesn't jump to a solution — asks clarifying questions first to validate the problem and scope. Comfortable pushing back to help a customer realize what they actually need.
- Strong technical fundamentals (Python, SQL ~8/10) plus agentic coding (Claude Code). Believes fundamentals matter because you must be able to evaluate what an agent generates.
- Values managers who have a growth plan for their reports and know where they want to be in 3 years.
- Bias toward solving recurring problems once, for everyone, rather than one-off fixes.

## How Rohit thinks about agent architecture
An agent needs three things: **Knowledge** (bounded, governed, certified context — a sales agent should only see sales data), **Tools** (MCPs to connect to data sources), and **Skills** (orchestration / how the agent should behave). He sees Snowflake Cortex and Databricks Genie as the same underlying idea (bring your semantic, get talk-to-data), with the key limits being context bootstrapping, production readiness thresholds, and portability of the semantic layer across agents.
